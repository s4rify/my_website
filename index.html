About Me

I am a PhD student researching advanced signal processing method for time series data. Currently, I am working in a neuropsychology work group in Oldenburg where I investigate anomaly detection methods for data recorded from the brain in everyday situations. My main research interest lies in the advancement of signal processing systems which can detect and decode brain signals using mobile EEG hardware. During my PhD project, I developing a training and communication system for severely brain-damaged patients in collabroation with three universities and two partners from the industry. Here, I am involved in the design and production of new electrode hardware and in the software development. The project is funded by the Federal Ministry of Education and Research: NeuroCommTrainer .

Projects

SCALA

In the field of neuroergonomics, everything is about gaining insights in the human brain and its workings during natural motion, outside of traditional laboratory settings. We added to this field by developing an Android framework for the online processing of EEG or other time series data. SCALA uses the LabStreamingLayer to simultaneously process different data types on standard smartphones. It can be used for Brain Computer Interface experiments and is an open source project.

View Project

Riemannian Artifact Subspace Reconstruction

A big part of working on mobile data recordings are methods for the detection of artifacts in the data. In a recent project, I investigate whether the online artifact correction method Artifact Subspace Reconstruction can be improved by using Riemannian geometry in its core computation steps. Spoiler alert: it can indeed. The Matlab toolbox is an open source project.
