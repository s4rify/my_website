About Me

I am a PhD student researching advanced signal processing method for time series data. Currently, I am working in a neuropsychology work group in Oldenburg () where I investigate anomaly detection methods for brain data. My main research interest lies in the advancement of signal processing systems which can detect and decode brain signals using mobile EEG hardware. During my PhD project, I am helping to develop a training and communication system for severely brain-damaged patients. Here, I am involved in the design and production of new electrode hardware and in the software development. The project is funded by the Federal Ministry of Education and Research (NeuroCommTrainer, BMBF, https://www.bmbf.de/en/index.html).
